## Section 7: Summary

TALY is a small, stable managed IT environment — 7 desktops, ~5 users, 16 network devices — generating modest support volume of 54 tickets and 63.9 hours in 2025.

At $9,336 total spend (~$778/month), the per-user IT cost is approximately **$1,867/year**. This covers fully managed security (CrowdStrike + Huntress), backup, monitoring, patch management, pen testing, and support labor.

The primary optimization opportunities are:

- **Shift Labor to Overnight:** US Tech Support accounts for 45% of hours at $125/hr. The majority of TALY's work — QNAP maintenance, security scans, patching, agent updates — is proactive and can be performed during US overnight hours by the offshore team at $15/hr. This represents a potential **8x cost reduction** on shifted hours.
- **Minimize US Tech Support:** After the current contract cycle, US Tech Support should transition to **hourly billing only** — reserved for the rare escalation or onsite visit. This removes the contracted US Tech allotment (~$2,915/yr) from the monthly invoice, replacing it with pay-per-use at $125/hr only when needed.
- **Move Offshore AH to NH:** The offshore AH hours ($30/hr, US daytime) should also be shifted to NH ($15/hr, US overnight) wherever possible. Most offshore work for TALY is already proactive maintenance — firmware upgrades, sync verifications, security scans — that does not require real-time user interaction.
- **Device Count Reduction:** The reduction from 9 to 7 desktops decreases per-device service costs by ~$50/month. Ensure billing reflects the current 7-desktop count.
- **Leverage Low Volume:** At ~5 hours/month average, TALY does not need significant contracted labor. A small offshore overnight allotment plus hourly US Tech billing is the most cost-effective model.

## Section 8: 2026 Budget Projections

### 8.1 Cost Optimization Strategy

The 2026 strategy for TALY focuses on three levers to reduce costs:

**1. Move Work to Overnight ($15/hr)**

The bulk of TALY's support work is proactive — QNAP maintenance, security assessments, firmware upgrades, patching, and agent updates. None of this requires real-time user interaction. By routing these tasks to the offshore overnight team (India daytime = US overnight at $15/hr), the cost per hour drops from $30–$125 down to $15.

**2. Minimize US Tech Support**

After the current contract cycle, US Tech Support transitions from contracted to **hourly billing only**. This eliminates the ~$173/month contracted US Tech allotment. US Tech is reserved for:
- Onsite visits (hardware, network)
- Complex escalations requiring US-based expertise
- Billed at $125/hr remote or $150–200/hr onsite, only when used

**3. Keep Per-Device Costs Low**

The reduction from 9 to 7 desktops saves approximately $50–60/month on per-device services. Pricing increases on some services (Huntress, SI, PMW, Pen Testing) partially offset this reduction.
