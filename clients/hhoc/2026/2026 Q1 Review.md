## Section 5: Notable Trends & Concerns

### 5.1 Items Requiring Attention

**1. Email & M365 Concentration Risk**
46.5 hours (67.4% of Q1) on Email & M365 is unsustainable. This could indicate:
- A complex M365 migration or reconfiguration in progress
- Recurring issues with a specific M365 service (Exchange, Teams, SharePoint)
- Inadequate M365 configuration requiring repeated intervention
- User training gaps driving repeat requests

**Recommendation:** Root-cause analysis on the top Email & M365 tickets is needed. If a systemic issue is driving repeat work, resolving it could save 20-30 hours per quarter.

**2. Proactive Maintenance Gap**
The drop from 35.5% to 13.0% proactive work means less preventive maintenance is being performed. Deferred patching, monitoring, and security maintenance creates compounding risk over time.

**3. Hours per Ticket Elevated**
At 1.28 hrs/ticket (Q1 avg), issues are taking 45% longer to resolve than the 2025 average of 0.88. While March showed improvement (0.94), the Q1 average remains elevated.

**4. US Support Mix Shift**
US-based support increased from 28.4% to 38.3% of hours. If this trend continues, it will increase the effective blended rate. Monitor whether the staffing transition is driving temporary US-heavy allocation or if HHOC's issues genuinely require more US-based attention.

**5. Weekend Coverage Decrease**
Weekend entries dropped to 12.2% of total entries vs. a more robust presence in 2025. For a smaller account this may be normal variation, but it should be monitored.
