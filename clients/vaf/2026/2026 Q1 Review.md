## Section 4: Notable Trends & Observations

### 4.1 Server Management Escalation

The most actionable trend in Q1. Server Management tickets are on pace to double the 2025 total (projected ~100 vs. 48 actual). At 1.07 hours per ticket, these are also among the most time-intensive. A root cause review should determine whether this is driven by:

- Aging server hardware requiring more frequent intervention
- Changes in workload or configuration
- Recurring issues on specific servers
- Expanded server monitoring catching issues previously undetected

### 4.2 Offshore Leverage Maximized

The near-elimination of US Tech Support hours (0.2 hrs in Q1 vs. ~27 hrs in 2025) represents a significant operational shift. The offshore team is successfully handling the full breadth of support without escalation to US-based resources. This keeps the effective support cost low but reduces the US-based knowledge of the VAF environment.

### 4.3 Patch Complexity Increasing

Average patch resolution time increased 44% (0.52 → 0.75 hrs/ticket). While patch ticket counts are down as a percentage of total work, each patch is taking longer. This could reflect Windows cumulative update complexity, application patching requirements, or servers requiring more manual patch intervention.

### 4.4 Consistent Monthly Volumes

Monthly ticket counts of 41, 42, and 41 across Q1 demonstrate highly predictable support demand. This consistency makes capacity planning straightforward and indicates no unusual environmental changes or disruptions during the quarter.

### 4.5 Hours per Ticket Trend

The gradual increase in hours per ticket across Q1 (0.68 → 0.73 → 0.75) is minor but worth monitoring. If this trend continues through Q2, it could indicate growing complexity in the ticket mix or an environment requiring more hands-on attention per issue.
